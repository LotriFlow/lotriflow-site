What Happens When You Quit Smoking? (Track with our App)

Your body is incredibly resilient. The moment you stop smoking, it begins to heal. Here's a complete timeline of the amazing health benefits you'll experience — all tracked automatically in the SmokeFree Journey app.

20 Minutes

Heart Rate Stabilizes

Pulse and blood pressure start to settle.

8 Hours

Carbon Monoxide Falling

Oxygen levels rising as CO leaves your blood.

12 Hours

CO Near Normal

Blood oxygen back toward normal ranges.

24 Hours

Nicotine Clearing

Most nicotine is gone; cravings can still peak days 1–3.

48 Hours

Taste & Smell Improving

Nerve endings start recovering.

72 Hours

Breathing Getting Easier

Bronchial tubes begin to relax.

1 Week

1 Week In

Craving intensity often starts to ease.

2 Weeks

Circulation Boost

Blood flow and endurance improving.

3 Weeks

Brain Rebalancing

Nicotine receptors down-regulating.

1 Month

1 Month Milestone

Lung function beginning to improve.

2 Months

2 Months Smoke-Free

Energy and stamina continue to rise.

3 Months

3 Months Strong

Breathing and circulation meaningfully improved.

6 Months

6 Months

Coughing and wheezing often reduced.

9 Months

9 Months

Cilia recovery lowers lung infection risk.

1 Year

1 Year Smoke-Free

Heart disease risk may be significantly reduced.

2 Years

2 Years

Cardiovascular health often continues improving.

5 Years

5 Years

Stroke risk may approach non-smoker levels.

10 Years

10 Years

Long-term lung health significantly improved.

15 Years

Heart Disease Risk Equals Non-Smoker

Your risk of coronary heart disease is now equal to that of someone who has never smoked. Congratulations!
